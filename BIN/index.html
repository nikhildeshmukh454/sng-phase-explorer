Real Gas Behaviour of CO₂ using Peng–Robinson EOS

About the Equation

The Peng–Robinson Equation of State (PR EOS) is a cubic equation used to model the real gas behaviour of fluids, especially near critical conditions. Unlike the ideal gas law, it accounts for intermolecular attractions and finite molecular volume.

In this visualiser, the PR EOS is used to study the thermodynamic behaviour of Carbon Dioxide (CO₂), a widely used industrial fluid in refrigeration, supercritical extraction, and energy systems.

What do the plots show?

  • P–V Diagram: Shows how pressure varies with molar volume at a fixed temperature, highlighting real gas deviations.
  • P–T Diagram: Shows the saturation pressure as a function of temperature, representing phase equilibrium behaviour.
